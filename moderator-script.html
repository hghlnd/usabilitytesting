Moderator Script

Introduction (5 minutes)

Welcome the Participant:
"Hi [Participant's Name], thank you for taking the time to join us today. My name is Oscar, and I’ll be moderating this session. Before we begin, let me explain a bit about how this test will work."

Purpose of the Test:
"Today, we’re testing the usability of the Khan Academy website. The goal is to understand how easy or difficult it is to use the website and identify areas for improvement. Please remember, this is not a test of your skills; it’s the website we’re evaluating. So feel free to be honest in your feedback. Your input is incredibly valuable."

Logistics:
"This session will take about 40 minutes. I’ll ask you to complete a few tasks on the website, and I’ll be observing how you interact with it. Don’t worry about making mistakes; they’re actually helpful for us to learn. If you have questions during the tasks, I may not answer right away because I want to see how you navigate the site independently. After each task, I’ll ask for your feedback."

Consent and Recording:
"This session will be recorded for research purposes only. The recordings are confidential and will not be shared outside the research team. Do I have your consent to proceed with the recording?"

Pre-Test Questions:
"Before we start, I have a few quick questions to understand your background better:"
- Have you used Khan Academy before?
- If yes, what do you typically use it for?
- How often do you use educational websites?
- On a scale of 1 to 5, how comfortable are you with technology (1 being not comfortable at all, and 5 being very comfortable)?

Tasks (35 minutes)

  1. Sign-Up Process (5 minutes):
    "Imagine you’re a new user who wants to create an account on Khan Academy. Your task is to sign up for a new account using the website or mobile application."
  2. Homepage Navigation and Starting a Lesson (6 minutes):
    "Now that you’re signed in, imagine you want to start learning. Your task is to explore the homepage and start a lesson in a subject of your choice."
  3. Feedback and Progress Tracking (5 minutes):
    "While completing the lesson, take note of any feedback you receive and how your progress is tracked. Your task is to explore these features during the lesson."
  4. Switching Between Subjects (5 minutes):
    "While in the middle of your current lesson, imagine you want to switch to a different subject. Your task is to navigate to another subject and start a lesson there."
  5. Accessibility Features (6 minutes):
    "Imagine you need to adjust the accessibility settings, such as changing text size or enabling captions. Your task is to find and use the accessibility features on the platform."
  6. Search Functionality (5 minutes):
    "Imagine you’re looking for resources on a specific topic, such as the Pythagorean Theorem. Your task is to use the search bar to find relevant lessons or content."
  7. Account Recovery (3 minutes):
    "Imagine you’ve forgotten your password and need to reset it. Your task is to go through the account recovery process."
  8. Mobile vs. Desktop Comparison (5 minutes):
    "Now, imagine you’re using Khan Academy on a mobile device. Your task is to start a lesson and complete a quiz."

Wrap-Up and Feedback (5 minutes)

General Feedback Questions:
"Thank you for completing all the tasks. I’d like to ask a few final questions about your experience today:"
1. What did you like most about using Khan Academy?
2. What did you find most frustrating or confusing?
3. Are there any features you think could be improved or added?
4. Would you recommend this platform to others? Why or why not?

Closing:
"That’s the end of our session. Thank you so much for your time and valuable feedback—it will help us improve the platform for all users. If you have any follow-up questions, feel free to contact me at [Your Email]. Have a great day!"
